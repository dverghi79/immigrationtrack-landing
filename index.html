The Problem

Immigration attorneys track deadlines in spreadsheets. Cases slip through.

When you're managing 60+ concurrent H-1B and PERM cases, every deadline is a potential malpractice claim. Spreadsheets don't send alerts. Calendars don't know USCIS processing times. And your paralegal can only check so many cases per day.

Missed RFE windows

USCIS gives you 87 days to respond to an RFE. When you're juggling 50+ cases, that window closes faster than you expect. One missed RFE means re-filing, months of delay, and a client whose visa status is at risk.

Deadline calculation errors

H-1B validity periods, I-94 expirations, PERM recruitment timelines, 240-day rules. Each case type has different deadline logic. Manual tracking means a single miscalculation can blow up an entire case.

No pipeline visibility

Your H-1B cap cases, PERM cases, and I-140s all live in different tabs. You can't see which cases need attention this week without checking every spreadsheet row manually. Urgent items hide in plain sight.

How It Works

From case intake to automatic deadline tracking in minutes.

Step 1

Add a case

Enter the case type (H-1B, PERM, I-140, etc.), client details, and filing date. ImmigrationTrack automatically calculates every downstream deadline based on USCIS processing rules.

Step 2

Deadlines auto-populate

RFE response windows, I-94 expirations, PERM recruitment period start/end dates, and status validity dates are all calculated and added to your dashboard. No manual date entry.

Step 3

Get alerted before anything is due

Receive email and dashboard alerts at 30, 14, and 7 days before each deadline. Urgent items surface to the top. Your weekly summary shows exactly which cases need attention this week.
